What it detects

Core checks on whether the body is where the estimator thinks it is

Every threshold is read from config.yaml, so warning and danger bands are tuned per vehicle and per dive.

Excessive roll

Rotation about x (forward). Sustained roll usually means trim or thruster imbalance.

warn 10° · danger 25°

Excessive pitch

Rotation about y (left). Nose-up/down beyond band points to ballast or pitch trim.

warn 8° · danger 20°

Abnormal yaw rate

Rotation rate about z (up). Flags spins and uncommanded heading drift.

warn 30°/s

IMU vs GTSAM

Raw IMU attitude against the state estimator. Disagreement is the early sign of divergence.

warn 7° · danger 15°

Mounting offset

Static roll/pitch bias between the IMU and base_link, recovered from a level sample.

est. calibrate → calibration.yaml

Estimator divergence

Large mismatch can indicate estimator divergence, especially if it persists across a log.

indicated by IMU/GTSAM mismatch

It checks roll, pitch, yaw rate, and IMU/GTSAM estimator disagreement while

IMU alignment

A still, level sample reveals how the IMU is really mounted

An IMU bolted a few degrees off from base_link reports a tilt that was never there. Blue Horizon separates the mounting bias from real motion so warnings can track the vehicle rather than the bracket when calibration is supplied.

  1. Sit the AUV still and level on the bench or cradle.
  2. Record a short sample and run calibrate.
  3. Any residual roll/pitch is treated as the mounting offset.
  4. Offsets are written to calibration.yaml and subtracted when analysis is run with --calibration calibration.yaml.
calibration.yaml — recovered offsets
roll+4.2° observed→roll_offset_deg: -4.20
pitch-2.8° observed→pitch_offset_deg: 2.80
yawnot estimated (level sample)
applied by passing --calibration calibration.yaml to analyze/report
CLI reference

The whole surface, exactly

analyze <csv> --config fScan a log and print warning / danger events with timestamps plus a summary.
report <csv> --format markdownPrint the summary report without the event stream.
calibrate <csv>Estimate IMU mounting offsets from a still, level sample.
--config config.yamlLoad per-vehicle warning and danger thresholds.
--calibration calibration.yamlApply roll/pitch mounting offsets before evaluating attitude.
--output calibration.yamlChoose where the calibrate command writes offsets.
Frame safety

Don't trust a warning until the frame is verified

Most false AUV faults aren't faults — they're frames. Blue Horizon prints its assumptions at startup and refuses to mix conventions silently.

A warning is only as honest as the frame it was computed in. Verify the convention, then believe the number.
— engineering rule, project spec
✓
ENU vs NED
Up-vs-down sign flips invert roll and pitch wholesale.
✓
Quaternion ordering
w-first vs w-last yields a plausible but wrong attitude.
✓
Degrees vs radians
A 57× scale error reads as instant, total divergence.
✓
IMU ≠ base_link
Mounting offset masquerades as a real, constant tilt.
✓
Timestamp mismatch
Misaligned clocks fake mismatch between IMU and estimator.
✓
Sign convention
A flipped axis turns left roll into right and hides faults.
